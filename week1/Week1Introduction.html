Week 1 - Immersive Worlds:

“Human beings have been creating and using themed environments for millennia. Over 32,000 years ago, the cave painters at Lascaux used effects from illustration animated by flickering firelight, focused attention, natural acoustics, music, manipulation of space, and narration to create a virtual reality environment. That was a bordered system-the creators shaped everything within its boundaries to a purposed effect. The technology changes and the stories evolve, but the way people interpret sensory input does not. Culture is the software of the mind and designers need to understand-at a conscious level-how culture affects not only how we interpret what we see, hear, feel, and smell, but how that influence focuses our attention so we may not even ‘see’ the design because our minds are focused elsewhere.” - Margaret J. King

Experience building:

“Environmental Mindfulness” or the practice of taking a hundred mental pictures a day of scenes and experiences. “What is it about a space that gives it its character? What is unique about a particular experience?” Game of improvising spaces, experiences, progressions, collective improv, etc.

Final Project:

Contribute to a collaborative world(s) or experience as a class. We plan to make an artifact or living demo of this work that can be accessed in the future- imagine this as an exhibition or group work combining multiple modalities: sight, sound, writing, architecture, performance, etc.  Related term: “Gesamtkunstwerk”  or “total artwork.”
