Virtual Reality and Digital Spaces

MMART-170


This course introduces historical, theoretical, and practical tools for the creation of Virtual Reality experiences and Digital Worlds. We provide a number of interviews  by practitioners working in immersive media arts. We will focus on the question, “What is Virtual Reality today and what can it become in the future?”

Instructors

Joshua Dickinson  

Dr. Gustavo Alfonso Rincon

Week 1 - Introduction:

Introduction to the course topics, goals, and areas of exploration. Brief history of virtual reality both as an idea and applied technology. “What is VR and what is its potential?” Explanation of the Final Project where we aim to build a collaborative world/exhibition/experience as a class.

Week 2 - Introduction to Generative AI:

Introducing contemporary and emerging generative AI tools and methodologies. “How can AI enhance and increase creative output?” Discussion of tools such as Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, Polycam, Skybox.AI, Infinigen, RVC, MusicGen, and ChatGPT.

Weeks 3-4 - Sound Design:

Two weeks covering sound design, reactive audio, and generative tools in the sonic domain. Students learn about spatialization, 3D audio, and how to create immersive auditory experiences. Overview of standard audio processing techniques, effects, and synthesis.

Week 5 - Game engines and graphics:

Game engines and graphical techniques are demonstrated: 360-degree experiences, immersive worlds, and WebVR, lighting, materials, shaders, rendering, dynamic environments, events, and scene-building.

Week 6 - Worldbuilding and Experience Crafting:

Focus on Worldbuilding and crafting experiences. Students will explore the psychology of immersion, hard vs. soft worldbuilding, and the application of these concepts in VR.

Week 7 - Contemporary VR Toolkits:

This week introduces contemporary VR toolkits in Unity, Unreal, and PlayCanvas. We will discuss Vision OS and Apple's “Spatial Computing” SDKs.

Week 8 - Input and Control:

Overview of input and control mechanisms in VR, including hand tracking, motion tracking, and foveated rendering. We will explore speculative and experimental possibilities in human-computer interaction (HCI).

Week 9 - Environmental Storytelling:

This week covers environmental storytelling, architectural connections, and capturing environments through photogrammetry. It introduces NERFs and other technologies.

Weeks 10-11 - Collaborative Worldbuilding:

These weeks explore collaborative and improvisatory worldbuilding, introducing Universal Scene Description (USD), PS4 Dreams, and the Metaverse. The focus is on crafting worlds from within, speaking or dreaming the world into existence, and the concept of “trading experiences.” We will continue to expand on the techniques we have learned, building more resources and preparing to place them into our final project exhibition space.

Week 12 - Final Project:

We will finish  building a collective VR experience that serves as a living class artifact. Each student will contribute to an online exhibition space, while creating documentation for personal portfolios and possibly a group publication after the end of the semester.
